THE GODS

The story of the Gods begins when Ometéotl, God of creation and the representation of fertility, creates the four principal deities of the Nahuatl pantheon and with it, the creation of life. Each of these deities were the representation of the cardinal points; north, south, east and west.They also had a color assigned to each of them; white, black, red and blue.

OMETEOTL
In the Aztec creation story, Ometecuhtli was self-birthed, and as such the androgynous being’s dual genders acted as husband and wife to give birth to the other four major Aztec gods—Huitzilopochtli, Quetzalcoatl, Tezcatlipoca, and Xipe Totec, who in turn represented the four cardinal sides
QUETZALCOATL
Quetzalcoatl, regarded as the son of the primordial god Ometecuhtli, was venerated as the creator of mankind and earth. The very name ‘Quetzalcoatl’ comes from the combination of the Nahuatl words for the quetzal—the emerald plumed bird, and coatl which translates as serpent. As for his aspects, often considered the Aztec god of wind and rain, Quetzalcoatl also espoused a variety of avenues like science, agriculture, crafts, and even merchants.
TEZCATLIPOCA
The lord of the nocturnal sky and the eternal antithesis of his brother Quetzalcóatl. Tezcatlipoca, among the major Aztec gods and goddesses, is also sometimes credited as being the co-creator of the world, ‘Smoking Mirror’ in Nahuatl.
HUITZILIPOCHTLI
Considered one of the major Aztec gods, Huitzilopochtli was also the supreme deity of the Mexica people (whose nobles later formed the Aztec Empire). In essence, as opposed to many other Aztec gods and goddesses, Huitzilopochtli was intrinsically a Mexica deity rather uninfluenced by earlier Mesoamerican divine entities.
XIPE TOTEC
A deity of agricultural renewal, vegetation, seasons, goldsmiths, and liberation, Xipe Totec was counted among the major Aztec gods and goddesses. And while his related concepts and powers seem fairly innocuous, the worship of Xipe Totec was anything but.
