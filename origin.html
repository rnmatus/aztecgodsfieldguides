ORIGIN

THE CREATION

Aztec culture was very vast with the creation of the Gods; they practically had one for every moment of their life or everyday activities. Some of the Gods were aquatic, such as Acuecucyoticihuati the goddess of the seas and rivers, meanwhile others like Huitzilopochtli were adored to maintain the sun firm on the horizon everyday. In the first sky the Moon and the clouds move. Here lives Meztli, the Moon; Tlazolteotl, the filth eater; Tiacapan, the older sister, the one she guides; Ixcuina, the one that removes her face; Tecotzin or Teicu, the youngest. All of them represent the phases of the Moon.

THE UNDERWORLD

This site has nothing to do with the Christian hell, although it is below the earth like that one, it is the place where all the beings who have died have to go to discarnate and for their soul to reach its most pure. Depending on the way they die, they can go to different places where they will reach eternal rest.
The underworld is made up of nine levels starting with the one just below the earth’s surface. On the first level newcomers ford a river with the help of a dog, hence the custom of venerating them and treating them with respect. The second layer has two large mountains that approach and move away preventing the dead from passing.

THE SUNS

Underlying Aztec religious beliefs was the Legend of the Suns, the explanation of the origin of the universe. According to legend, the universe had been created and destroyed four previous times, and each creation formed an age called a “sun.” The fifth epoch began in darkness. The gods gathered at Teotihuacan, and two of them sacrificed themselves by jumping into a fire and rising as the sun and the moon. The remaining gods then sacrificed themselves, their blood setting the sun and moon in motion.
